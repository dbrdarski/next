## 2. The length derivation — Λ-semantics with exactness stamps **[rebuilt, round 1]**

**The reference set:** `Λ(T) = { |t| : t ∈ ⟦T⟧ }`. **`len(T)` returns a stamped pair `(NumberContract, Exact | Approx)`** with the soundness law `Λ(T) ⊆ ⟦len(T).contract⟧` always, and the `Exact` stamp claiming `⟦len(T).contract⟧ = Λ(T)`, provenly. Uninhabited T has `Λ(T) = ∅` and `len(T) = (Bottom, Exact)` — impossible shapes are never realizable lengths.

- **Exact tuples:** `len(Tuple(e₀…e_{k−1}))` = `(Bottom, Exact)` when the tuple is **refuted uninhabited** (some element proven empty — RC productivity); `(Equals(k), Exact)` when **proven inhabited**; `(Equals(k), Approx)` when inhabitation is **unproven**. **Record counts, stated separately [round 2]:** exact records — refuted uninhabited → `(Bottom, Exact)`; proven inhabited → `(Equals(n), Exact)`; unproven → `(Equals(n), Approx)`. Open records — `(Bottom, Exact)` / **`(GE(n), Exact)`** / `(GE(n), Approx)` respectively; the open-`Exact` claim assumes arbitrary additional distinct fields are constructible — any field-name domain restriction or sealing demotes it to `Approx`.
- **Concat:** the C§7 sum of segment lengths; stamp `Exact` iff every operand is `Exact` **and** every applied C§7 rule is exact for its pair — any `Approx` operand or approximate rule (gcd-lattice coarsening included) stamps the result `Approx`.
- **Union:** union of branch lengths; `Exact` iff all branches `Exact` (the union of exact sets is exact).
- **Recursion — the linear fragment solved as a weighted graph [rebuilt, round 2; the scalar collapse was the blocker]:** the exact solution applies when every recursive alternative contains at most one own-SCC reference **and the label boundary holds** — every recursive-edge increment and every base is a **finite exact length set** (`Equals` and **bounded `Range`** labels are admitted after finite enumeration [0.3.1 — a bounded range *is* a finite exact set]; `GE`, nonfinite modular labels, and infinite increment languages like `Concat(OneOrMore(E), R)`'s {1,2,3,…} drop the system to `Approx` — sound upper bound — unless the recorded weighted-semilinear upgrade is implemented). **The solver preserves SCC members as states:** each member is a graph state; each recursive alternative a directed edge weighted by its nonrecursive length contribution; base alternatives contribute accepting lengths at their own states. Per-state solutions are ultimately periodic with **a valid eventual period = the gcd of the weights of the SCC's productive closed walks — equivalently, the SCC-wide cycle-weight gcd — never the gcd of individual transition weights** [0.3.1: “cycles through that state” invited a simple-cycles-only misreading; a nonminimal valid period is acceptable, since the per-state residue sets and exceptional prefixes preserve the exact language] (the R/S counterexample: two weight-1 edges, cycle weight 2; `Λ(R)` = evens, `Λ(S)` = odds; edge-gcd 1 would erase the parity — TL-19). Exceptional sets and the tail bound come from a bounded per-state saturation, its bound computed in advance from the finite label sets (Principle 7); single-member single-increment systems recover the `Mod(k, base)` special case; the multi-increment case ({2,3} over {0} → `Union(Equals(0), GE(2))`, exact) unchanged. `Mod(g, {r₁…rₙ})` is **notation** for `Union(Mod(g, r₁)…)` — no new Number constructor [round 2]. **Nonlinear alternatives stay admissible**; their lengths are `(GE(structural minimum), Approx)` — sound, subcontract-witness-powerless per §3, and still able to refute by disjoint uppers where the bound bites.

## 3. Refutation discipline and the reverse transfer **[round 1]**

**Approximate lengths never refute subcontracts.** The rules: **(i)** disjoint length contracts refute **intersection emptiness** even when both are `Approx` — upper approximations of disjoint sets are sound disjointness evidence; **(ii)** a **subcontract refutation** requires a **realizable source-length witness** — a length from an `Exact` derivation, or one carried by an explicit inhabitant (productivity witnesses supply these) — excluded by the target's sound upper bound; **(iii)** absent such a witness, the verdict is **unproven** — an `Approx`-source mismatch (the gcd-coarsened `GE(0)` containing the unrealizable 1) proves nothing; **(iv)** a **length-based** proof of arity inclusion requires an `Exact` target length language; an `Approx` target is a necessary filter only — and **independent structural proofs remain available**: reflexivity (`T ⊑ T`) and the constructor rows never depend on length exactness [scoped, round 2]; **(v)** stated precisely [round 2]: `Approx` lengths **cannot supply subcontract refutation witnesses, but may refute intersection emptiness through disjoint upper bounds**.

**`restrictLen(T, D)` — the defined reverse transfer** (replacing v0.1's "inverting §2," which named no operation): semantic requirement `⟦restrictLen(T, D)⟧ = { t ∈ ⟦T⟧ : |t| ∈ ⟦D⟧ }`, or a **marked sound over-approximation** of that set within ⟦T⟧. Structural rules: exact tuples filter by membership (keep or Bottom); `Repeat(E)` against `GE(n)` unrolls — `Concat(Tuple(E×n), Repeat(E))`; Unions distribute; Concat distributes D over segment-length allocations **when at most one variable segment leaves the allocation determined** (the alignment discipline, reused); recursive contracts take certified unfoldings for demand-depth-bounded D. Everything else returns the **derived contract form `LengthRestricted(T, D)`** [round 2 — replacing the unclosed obligation-tuple]: denotation **exact by definition**, `{ t ∈ ⟦T⟧ : |t| ∈ ⟦D⟧ }`; hence `Λ(LengthRestricted(T, D)) = Λ(T) ∩ ⟦D⟧`, and its `len` is the stamped intersection — `Exact` whenever `len(T)` is; otherwise `Approx` **unless an independent rule proves the intersection exact**, the disjointness-to-Bottom case included [0.3.1]. Lowering stays demand-directed — the structural rules above fire when their conditions arrive; unlowered, the form participates in subcontract and emptiness rows conservatively, three-voiced. It is **analyzer-derived, never user syntax**, so the C§9 admissibility perimeter is untouched; variance: **positive in T and monotone in D** under Number-contract inclusion [0.3.1]. **Canonical rows [0.3.1]:** `LengthRestricted(Bottom, D) → Bottom`; `LengthRestricted(T, Bottom) → Bottom`; `LengthRestricted(T, TopLength) → T`; `LengthRestricted(LengthRestricted(T, D₁), D₂) → LengthRestricted(T, D₁ ∩ D₂)`; `LengthRestricted(T, D) ⊑ T` — proven. `first = (...v) => v[0]` still rejects `first()` — now through a named operation rather than a gesture.

## 4. The segment-alignment procedure **[algorithmic, round 1]**

Deciding `Concat(S…) ⊑ Tuple(t₀…t_{k−1})` (and Concat-vs-Concat): **(1) forced boundaries** — peel an outer exact segment **only when the opposite shape and the current exact length constraints make its occupied window unique** [0.3.1 — forced, never merely outermost: a zero-or-more segment on the opposite side can shift an edge]; each peel binds positions; each peel consumes a segment and its positions (termination: bounded by segment count + arity). **(2) Interior:** at most one unresolved variable segment ⇒ it binds the residual window; solve its length equation against the residual (C§7); check elements per position. **(3) Variable-vs-variable residuals ⇒ unproven** unless `Exact` lengths pin a unique split. **Witness shapes:** a proof is the alignment map (segment → position window, plus per-position element proofs); a refutation is either a realizable-length witness (§3.ii) or an **`ElementRefutation { alignment, sourceShapeWitness, position, sourceElementWitness, targetElementExclusion }`** [round 2] — the positional mismatch alone is insufficient: if any *other* required source position, or the selected union/recursive branch, is uninhabited, the source is empty and the inclusion is **true** (`Tuple(Number, U)` vs `Tuple(String, Top)` with U possibly-empty — round one's uninhabited-shape error, reappearing through alignment); the shape witness must realize the **same branch** the mismatching position was projected from, and a complete concrete source tuple always suffices. TL-01 splits accordingly [round 1]: **TL-01a** "spread-call *arity* accept" (lengths only); **TL-01b** full `Apply` acceptance with compatible element contracts stated.

## 5. Strings — boundary-state seams **[rebuilt, round 1; the interval was unsound]**

The `−1` interval is withdrawn: extended grapheme clustering can merge across the seam by **more than one** — the leading-ZWJ counterexample (`👩` + `ZWJ·👩·ZWJ·👧`: 1 + 2 → 1) is now TL-09's flagship. The sound derivation is the **boundary-state summary**: a string or string contract is summarized as the **compositional transition summary [strengthened, round 2 — independently computed suffix/prefix states are too weak: the right operand's segmentation depends on the incoming state, RI parity the plain example]**: `summary(s) : IncomingState → finite map of OutgoingState → stamped NumberContract` — the count-delta *language* per state pair; deltas over string contracts may be unbounded, so the output is a Number abstraction, never a literal enumeration [0.3.1] — over the pinned segmenter's finite state space; **concatenation is composition of summaries** — compositional, exact for literal–literal, sound over unions, seam effects mechanically derived, Unicode-re-pin invalidation built in (C§13.4), no guessed constant anywhere. **Soundness is checked exhaustively over the generated finite transition table; property testing remains an implementation cross-check, never the proof** [round 2]. Literal-literal concatenation recounts exactly. The proven-zero case stays exact (0.1.1). Counts still flow through C§7 afterward — the family's one-arithmetic principle survives; only the seam function is segmenter-owned.

## 6. Slices and rows

Slice lengths are **the normalized image of the piecewise clamp arithmetic** — no exactness folklore [rounds 1–2: clamping can map a **non-singleton** exact input to a singleton — stamps and cardinality are separate axes — `len ⊑ Range(2,5)`, `t[-2...]` gives `min(2, len)` = **`Equals(2)`**, the corrected TL-06]. Emptiness and subcontract rows run on §3's discipline: disjoint uppers refute intersections; subcontract refutations carry realizable witnesses; alignment per §4. Partition identity, notation corrected: `len(t[…k]) + len(t[k…]) = len(t)` — an equation over length contracts, with the tuple-level identity written over the slices' *contracts*, never length objects in tuple seats.

## 7. Perimeter

Nonlinear recursive lengths: admissible contracts, `Approx`-only lengths — exactness is the recorded upgrade (the general unary/semilinear solver). Length multiplication: Part D's, unchanged. The seam bound: segmenter-derived, version-pinned, harness-verified — never a spec constant. Discharge moduli: C§7's per-pair tables (0.1.1) and RC's productivity procedure (which §2's inhabitation stamps consume).
