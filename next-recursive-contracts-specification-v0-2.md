## 1. Constructor — none; plus the admissibility laws **[round 1]**

A recursive contract is an ordinary named binding mentioning itself or its mutual group — late binding, no special form (E11). It must pass two admissibility checks, run on the canonical graph (§2); violations are **definition errors**:

**(a) Positivity.** Recursive references are admissible only where every path from the group binder to the occurrence is positive. Variance table: `Union`/`Intersection` positive in all branches; `Record` fields and `Tuple` elements positive; `Difference(B, E)` positive in B, **negative in E**. **v1 perimeter (the regular-shape fragment):** recursive references are admitted only beneath positive `Tuple`/`Record`/`Union`/`Intersection` structure; `Difference` exclusions and opaque predicates must be recursion-free; any future constructor declares its variance before recursion may pass through it. `Bad = Difference(Top, Bad)` is rejected — its operator is antitone, the approximants oscillate, and no least fixpoint exists to denote.

**(b) Structural guardedness.** Every cycle through the group's reference graph must cross a value constructor that descends to a proper structural subvalue — a `Tuple` element or `Record` field. `Union`/`Intersection` branching, aliasing, and μ-head traversal are **not** progress. `R = R` and `R = Union(Number, R)` are rejected with hints (the latter: it already denotes `Number` — write that). Rationale: positivity makes the group operator monotone (the fixpoint exists); guardedness makes inclusion induction well-founded (§5).

They arise as before: user named contracts; conform Shapes; variable-length tuple contracts (rest parameters); E5's static-key recursive constructors.

## 3. Denotation — inductive, vector-valued **[generalized round 1]**

For an m-member group, the body operator is **F : P(Values)ᵐ → P(Values)ᵐ**, monotone by positivity **and ω-continuous because the admitted regular-shape grammar is finitary** (Union is finitary; finite Intersection is continuous over increasing chains; Tuple/Record constructors depend on finitely many immediate children; Difference and opaque occurrences are recursion-free by §1) — monotonicity alone gives Knaster–Tarski existence; continuity is what licenses the chain formula [premise added 0.2.1]: **lfp(F) = ⋃ₙ Fⁿ(⊥,…,⊥) componentwise**, with ⟦member i⟧ its i-th projection; the scalar formula is the m = 1 case. Data acyclicity (B3) keeps every inhabitant finite; hence membership is well-founded, and every refutation witness or inhabitant produced anywhere in this package is a **finite concrete value** (C§16's witness discipline unchanged).

## 4. Certified unfolding — scoped to its two sources **[terminology repaired round 1]**

"Certified unfolding" names exactly the two ruled cases: **concrete membership** (bound = the value's finite depth) and **demand-directed structural projection** (bound = the demand's structural depth); step counts computed in advance, never a cap; no derivable bound ⇒ unproven. **The abstract procedures below never materialize unfoldings** — they traverse the finite canonical μ-graph, and their advance bounds are graph-theoretic, computed before work begins: the pair×progress state space (§5), the productivity state space (§6), the product graph (intersection emptiness). Principle 7 holds identically in both regimes.

## 5. Subcontract — progress-guarded pair induction **[rebuilt round 1]**

To decide `A ⊑ B` over canonical graphs:

0. **Empty-source short-circuit:** if `Empty(A)` is proven (§6) → **proven** (Bottom is a subcontract of everything).
1. **Progress is per-hypothesis and depth-stamped [0.2.1 — the normative executable rule].** Maintain `sourceDepth`, incremented exactly by source-side Tuple-element and Record-field descent; Union/Intersection decomposition and μ-head traversal never increment. When a pair `(A, B)` is first assumed, record `assumedDepth[(A, B)] = sourceDepth`; a revisit closes as *holds* **only when `sourceDepth > assumedDepth[(A, B)]`** — equivalently, every closed cycle in the finite proof graph contains at least one source-side structural-progress edge. A global or inherited progress Boolean is **non-conforming**: it can leak progress made before a nested hypothesis was assumed (RC-16). *This guard is the induction:* reuse corresponds to a strictly smaller finite source value. (Explicitly not Algorithm B — function identity is coinductive over rational trees; inclusion is inductive over finite values; the guard is the entire difference.)
2. Otherwise decompose by the ordinary constructor rows (Union distributes; Record fieldwise per the openness rule; Tuple positionally), recursing with the updated state.
3. **Refutation requires an assembled witness.** A component mismatch alone never refutes: the verdict is *refuted* only when a **complete finite source witness in A ∖ B** has been assembled — the witness builder supplying inhabitants (from §6's stored fragments) for every other required source component. Where the source side cannot be proven inhabited, no counterexample exists — the sound outcomes are proven (via step 0) or unproven, never refuted. **Witness roles are distinct in the API [0.2.1]:** an *inhabitant witness* (productivity, §6) evidences membership of a contract; an *exclusion witness* evidences membership of A ∖ B; an inhabitant alone never establishes exclusion. Flagship: `μR.Record({next: R}) ⊑ Number` is **proven** — the source is empty; v0.1's "any component refuted → refuted" would have wrongly refuted it.

Termination — with a lemma admissibility supplies for free [0.2.1]: a proof cycle revisiting `(A, B)` means the source contract cycled; every admissible source cycle crosses a structural constructor (§1b); hence **every revisit arrives at strictly greater depth** — equal-depth revisits are impossible — and each pair expands exactly once over the finite pair space. Verdicts three-voice, cached per C§13.4 (proven/refuted permanent in-namespace; unproven rule-set-versioned). The false-inclusion trap `A = Union(Number, A) ⊑ B = B` cannot arise: both definitions are inadmissible (§1b) — and were they normalized instead, the guard would still block the unearned hypothesis closure.

## 6. Emptiness — bounded productivity closure **[rebuilt round 1]**

Over the **fully constructed productivity graph — M states, known in advance** (the canonical group's nodes plus the product states recursive Intersection introduces) [0.2.1], compute **productivity** monotonically: seed with primitively inhabited leaves (the exact emptiness fragment); `Union` is productive when any branch is; `Tuple`/`Record` when every required component is; `Intersection` per its ordinary exact emptiness row; a recursive reference reads its target slot's current state. Propagate unproductive → productive until stable: **each state flips at most once, so at most M discoveries occur — no iteration budget exists** (Principle 7). Witness fragments are stored at each flip and compose into finite inhabitants.

Verdicts: **productive → proven non-empty, with witness**; **not productive, with every leaf decision exact → proven empty**; **any dependency on an unproven leaf row → unproven**. **Exactness, scoped:** decidable-exact precisely on the admitted positive regular fragment whose reachable nonrecursive leaves **and required ordinary intersection/product rows** all lie in the exact emptiness fragment; an opaque leaf keeps the verdict three-voiced — recursion never settles what its leaves cannot. The one-equation cases are N = 1: the flagship pair stands (`Union(Null, Record({next: R}))` non-empty via Null; `Record({next: R})` empty — a list with no nil is uninhabited in acyclic data). The mutual case that breaks one-pass assume-empty — `A = Record({b: B})`, `B = Union(Null, Record({a: A}))` — closes correctly here: B flips via Null, then A flips via `{b: null}`; both non-empty, witnesses in hand. **Intersection emptiness of two recursive contracts = productivity over their finite product graph.**
